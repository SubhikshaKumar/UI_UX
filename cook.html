DISCOVER COOK

The Cook Islands are for you. Strung between French Polynesia and Samoa but with strong ties to New Zealand, the archipelago's 15 islands are known for their enticing aquamarine lagoons, palm-fringed beaches, and volcanic peaks. Best of all, the locals are among the friendliest in the South Pacific. Rarotonga is the main tourist hub, with its many resorts, lush peaks, and plentiful beaches. Aitutaki boasts the beauty of Bora Bora, without the price tag. Hibiscus-laced villages snuggle on the hillsides, and along its heavenly lagoon lie 21 motus or small islets, many within kayak distance of the resorts. Ensconce yourself here in an over-the-water bungalow or hole up in a rustic beach shack on a remote out island and live your Robinson Crusoe fantasies.

FIRE ON WATER NIGHT PADDLING

Enjoy an evening on the ocean on this small group tour. Watch the sun set over Rarotonga, then float above the water as the stars emerge. Get to know the Cook Islands’ unique marine life from the comfort of your paddle board or kayak. On your return to land, contrast life on the water with your guides’ authentic Cook Islands fire dance. No experience required: all participants get a watercraft lesson and proper equipment Ask your guide all about local history and geography on this small group tour Enjoy your choice of gear: select a single or double kayak or a paddle board Safety first: life jackets and reef shoes can be provided, and your guide is first-aid trained

PADDLE BOAT YOGA

Try this relaxing fusion activity in the shallow Muri Lagoon on Rarotonga’s eastern shores. Learn how to paddle board on entry-level boards, and then paddle a short distance to a quiet island venue where you’ll experience floating yoga class. Combining yoga instruction with paddle board coaching, this peaceful lesson casts both activities in a new light. Learn to paddle a stand-up board without the hassle of hiring gear Get away from the crowds while you get on your feet Easy for newcomers: practice on wide, stable boards Enjoy some ‘you time’: reconnect with yourself and with nature
